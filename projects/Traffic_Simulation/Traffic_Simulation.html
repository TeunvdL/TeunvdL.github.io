Traffic Micro-Simulation and Autonomous Vehicles

Project Overview

Duration: Six Months

Group: Autonomous and Complex Systems group, TU/e

Commissioned by: TNO

This project aimed to develop an accurate traffic simulation model to assess the impact of autonomous vehicles on traffic safety and flow. Real-world traffic data was collected to generate realistic distributions of key metrics. These distributions formed the basis for calibrating an agent-based traffic simulation model.

Technical Details

The project involved several stages of development and analysis:

  • Data Collection: Extracting real-world traffic data from sensors and observation tools.
  • Macro-Level Analysis: Generating realistic distributions for metrics like vehicle speed, spacing, and flow.
  • Simulation Calibration: Developing methods to tune agent-based simulation parameters to align with observed real-world data.
  • Safety and Traffic Flow Modeling: Modeling and analyzing the impact of integrating autonomous vehicles into traffic systems.

Challenges and Solutions

Key challenges included ensuring the accuracy of the real-world data, fine-tuning simulation parameters for high fidelity, and modeling autonomous vehicle behavior within mixed traffic systems. Solutions involved iterative testing, leveraging statistical tools for calibration, and incorporating validated behavior models for autonomous agents.

Project Results

The simulation model demonstrated how different levels of autonomous vehicle penetration affect traffic flow and safety. It provided valuable insights into potential bottlenecks and safety risks, offering recommendations for infrastructure adaptations and policy decisions.
